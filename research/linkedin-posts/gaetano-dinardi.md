## Post 1
Nearly all of the SEO/GEO problems I am working on today are category misalignment and mismatch problems, not ticky-tacky "optimization" tricks or GEO hacks. B2B SaaS brands that have been around for 10+ years have a digital footprint in a "legacy" category that's now unsexy. And now there's a new thing they need to become known for: Customer Service CRM becoming Intelligent CX Platform. Low Code Platform becoming Agentic Systems Platform. The problem is that AI retrieval often follows entity neighborhoods. So when SaaS brands evolve into new positioning, they need a full blown category attack plan with 50% of the effort allocated to on-site content and the other 50% allocated to offsite: analyst relations, guest posts, review site optimization, YouTube, inclusion on Forrester/Gartner reports for the new category. Instead, they launch one new product page with a press release and expect magic to happen. It just doesn't work.

## Post 2
One of the hardest things about "traffic going down" is managing the executive perception of it. Organic traffic is down 15% May vs April, but demo requests attributed to organic search are down by 30%. They will assume: something is wrong, traffic is down, and as a result we are losing leads. But here's what no one considered: the drop in demo requests is a normalization after a few unusually strong months. The biggest traffic declines were mostly from TOFU informational pages, not high-intent commercial pages. AI search leads doubled. It's on YOU, as the channel owner, to surface these insights, control the narrative, and manage the executive perception. If you don't, then good luck.

## Post 3
I had a great meeting with a founder after not seeing each other for a few years. We talked about fitness, health, the stock market, practical uses for AI in business, family, fatherhood. But one principle on leadership stood out. Be a leader that's focused on process improvement and inputs. Don't be a leader that's only focused on the numbers and outcomes. Just pressing your team about the numbers without auditing the process translates to anxiety and doesn't fix the root of the problem. Being a great leader is often a matter of understanding the process, not just being fixated on numbers.
