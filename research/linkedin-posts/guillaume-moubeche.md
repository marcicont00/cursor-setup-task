## Post 1
SpaceX just bought a $60B company without spending a single dollar. And the stock it paid with had already gained more than the entire price of the deal. In hours. On its first day of trading. Everyone is debating the wrong thing. They ask: "Is SpaceX really worth $2 trillion?" The better question: "What does that valuation let SpaceX do that it could not do last week?" Valuation is not the market's opinion of today's revenue, today's margins, today's assets. Valuation is a claim on the future. It is the market saying: we believe this company becomes something far bigger than what exists today. If you think SpaceX is a rocket company, the valuation looks insane. If you think SpaceX is a new kind of infrastructure company, the conversation changes.

## Post 2
The best thing I ever did for lemlist was stop being CEO. A few years ago, I would've never written that sentence. Like most founders, I believed the company needed me to keep growing. I was wrong. Today, lemlist is 150+ people across 18 countries. We've crossed $50M+ ARR. We've launched new products, made acquisitions, integrated teams, and kept growing. Most importantly: the company became stronger after I stepped down as CEO. Companies don't scale because founders work harder. They scale because exceptional people decide to build the future together.

## Post 3
Stripe has been "about to IPO" for four years and will probably wait a lot longer. The best companies in the world have figured out that staying private is a competitive advantage. No quarterly earnings calls. No analyst pressure to optimize for the next 90 days. No obligation to explain long-term bets to shareholders who think in quarters. When you're building something that takes a decade, public markets are a huge distraction. The most significant valuation expansion happens in private markets.
