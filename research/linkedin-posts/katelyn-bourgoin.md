## Post 1
I went bankrupt building a "dream" business (a VC-backed tech startup). I was way too far in when I realized it wasn't even my dream. Then years later, I nearly built the wrong business again with my media and education company, Why We Buy. I bought into the solopreneur hype and hustled to grow Why We Buy past $1M a year. On paper everything looked great. In reality I felt alone. And very confused. I thought this is what I was supposed to want. Why did it feel so hollow? Then in 2024 I read Luke Burgis' book Wanting. It finally gave me a name for what I'd been doing: memetic desire. We unconsciously absorb other people's wants and mistake them for our own. That's why we can achieve big things that leave us feeling empty.

## Post 2
I've built an audience of 300K+ across LinkedIn, X, and email. My advantage? I had a few, actually. Most people growing fast followings had an edge of some sort. 5 Audience Growth Advantages: Proof Advantage — when you've already done the thing at a high level, your track record does the credibility work for you. Beauty Advantage — the research is blunt: we pay more attention and trust attractive faces more. Time Advantage — the more time you have to create content, comment, and hone your craft, the faster you grow. Money Advantage — you're rich enough to hire an all-star content team. Insight Advantage — do you approach your work differently? Are you willing to think in public, even if it means people will scrutinize and disagree with you? The Insight Advantage is the one nobody's born with and nobody can buy.
