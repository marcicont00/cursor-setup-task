## Post 1
The question that always breaks the internet: can money buy happiness? I had a chat with Simon Swords, who bootstrapped Fundipedia for 20 years before selling it for a life changing exit last year. The day his deal closed, Simon had what he called "a big old cry." He told me it wasn't relief. It was 25 years of pain, self loathing, and overthinking everything finally letting go. The money landed, but the anxiety didn't leave. He said something that stuck with me: "Once you have money and options, you're not allowed to be sad." I realized that while money might be able to buy you happiness, it can't make you "not sad." That is something entirely different. Happiness isn't an asset you can buy. It's a learned skill. The exit is not the finish line. It's a different starting line for a new kind of adventure.

## Post 2
Chris Walker walked away from a $22M business because he wanted something different. He was writing some of the best B2B marketing content on the internet. Then he disappeared. When he came back, he wasn't writing about demand gen or marketing anymore. He was writing about burnout, inner work, and the programming that holds entrepreneurs back. Chris mapped out 10 dimensions of freedom: Time, Social, Creative, Financial, Health, Location, Purpose and Mission, Relationship, Mental, and Spiritual. He rated himself a 2 out of 10 on mental and time freedom while his business was thriving. We build the thing that's supposed to set us free and it quietly takes our freedom away instead. True freedom starts from within.
